# Muse → JARVIS: the full capability map

> First-principles enumeration of every functional capability a personal, local-first JARVIS (for one user) needs — across 8 domains, each tagged with importance and Muse's current standing. Produced by a 9-agent direction workflow (2026-06-03). This is the canonical map the autonomous loop steers by; the recommended frontier is **"close the loop": continuous + autonomous + reliable** (see the synthesis at the end).

Status legend: ✅ have · 🟡 partial · ❌ missing · ❓ unknown.

**Tally:** 129 capabilities — 90 ✅ have, 29 🟡 partial, 10 ❌ missing. The foundation is unusually complete; the gaps cluster on one theme (autonomy / continuity / reliability), not scattered.

## Synthesis — the JARVIS essence & where Muse stands

### The core must-haves that define a personal JARVIS

- Grounded cited recall from YOUR world — answers come from your own notes/files/calendar/people with the real source quoted, not from training memory
- Honest refusal (fabrication=0) — every claim cites a real source, weak grounding becomes 'I'm not sure', un-groundable claims are dropped by deterministic code
- Durable knowing-you — a typed self-model (preferences, vetoes, goals, schedule) plus episodic memory of past sessions that ages out stale guesses but keeps hard truths
- Earned proactivity — surfacing the right thing unprompted at the right moment (imminent events, voiced commitments, self-promised follow-ups), gated on confidence and with the discipline to stay silent
- Safe action toward the world — draft-first, recipient-resolved, fail-closed approval gates, every act logged and reversible; never an autonomous send
- Carry a multi-step goal to a VERIFIED done — decompose, execute, retry transient failures, replan on deviation, confirm the effect actually happened
- Felt continuity & presence — pick up where you left off, a consistent voice, low-friction instant access, visible receipts you can verify in the moment
- Private by construction — the entire loop (text, voice, memory) runs local with cloud egress refused in code: 'tell it everything, it can't tell anyone'
- Autonomous self-improvement — it gets better from your corrections on its own while you aren't watching, not only when you run a CLI command

### Where Muse is genuinely strong (have)

- Grounded cited recall + the fabrication=0 grounding gate (RGV 4-criterion verifier, enforceAnswerCitations, weak-verdict re-verification across recall/reflection/council) — this is genuinely best-in-class and is Muse's whole identity edge, proven by live batteries

### Recommended north-star

> Close the loop: turn Muse from a deep set of manually-invoked, one-shot atoms into an autonomous always-on companion. Concretely the theme is 'continuous + autonomous + reliable' — (1) ingestion that keeps syncing your world into the citable corpus on its own, feeding (2) a self-development daemon that distills/consolidates/reinforces from your corrections unattended, feeding (3) action that carries a multi-step goal to a VERIFIED done with retry and replan. Every slice still STRENGTHENS the grounding edge (the new autonomous behaviors must cite their sources and verify their effects) and PROVES it with a live battery — autonomy without the fabrication=0 discipline would betray the identity. Email features stay deferred; the prize is the leap from 'a tool I operate' to 'an assistant that runs beside me.'

### Honest closing take

Jinan, the honest read is that Muse's foundation is far stronger than most 'personal AI' projects ever reach: the grounding/citation edge is genuinely differentiated and code-enforced, the knowing-you substrate (typed model with decay, provenance, episodic memory) is deep, the trust gate set is comprehensive, and the positive-trigger proactivity loop already works with real calm-tech discipline. The weakness is not breadth — it's that almost everything is a manually-invoked, one-shot atom, and the same shape recurs in every domain: the self-development daemon only reflects (it doesn't distill/consolidate/reinforce on its own), action plans once and fails closed without verify-or-replan, and perception pulls snapshots instead of continuously syncing. So the gap between Muse-today and a real JARVIS is almost entirely 'close the loop': make ingestion continuous, make self-improvement autonomous, and make multi-step action reliably carry to a verified done. That single theme — continuous, autonomous, reliable — is where the loop should aim next, and the good news is most of the pieces already exist as CLI commands waiting to be wired together rather than built from scratch.
